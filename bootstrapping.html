Bootstrapping

Initially, Itch.io was a site designed for Corcoran's own use, and as it gained popularity Corcoran has needed to continually adapt in order to accommodate the users of his site. The humble start, using one’s own perspective to shape a site that would later be used by many, brings to mind Thierry Bardini’s Bootstrapping.

In Chapter Five, Bardini explains the development of the personal interface, where SRI staff worked to develop the NLS. The ways that SRI staff referenced older pieces of technology, such as using the teletype interface model, brings to mind the way Corcoran, on a smaller scale, took inspiration from the other sites around him.
Yet while Engelbart and his team were working to develop new technology, Corcoran is taking already existing systems and altering them to make them more accessible. Unlike on Steam which uses a closed system that requires payment and the games to be reviewed, there are no barriers to getting started on Itch.io. Itch has taken an existing closed system and made a more accessible alternative.
