Itch.io Audit
Sources

Leaf Corcoran

Itch.io is created by Leaf Corcoran , as a byproduct of another project. In 2013, Corcoran needed a place to put games he had made using a programming language he had created, called “Moonscript”. Initially, Itch.io was a site designed for Corcoran’s own use, as he was frustrated by the inaccessabilty of other game distribution platforms. Yet it soon gained popularity, and Corcoran has continually adapted and update features in order to accommodate the users of his site.

Itch.io is not the main source of income for Corcoran, who has a full time job as a software developer. He is not concerned about the money, keeping the site free to use and even at times paying out of his own pocket for it. Corcoran is very passionate about the games on the site, often working closely with its users to adjust aspects to their liking and getting attached to the games on the site, being invested in their success and upset when they don’t do well. He runs the site on his own, with a small team, and reports that it has essentially eaten up all of his free time. Yet he doesn’t seem too upset about this.
Corcoran’s approach to his site has been quite benevolent, Itch.io is clearly a labor of love. With the tech industry as it is now, Corcoran’s care and passion is refreshing and awe inspiring. It’s wonderful to see people who seem unconcerned with personal or financial gain, just doing things out of love for them.
